Bograch is a Hungarian dish characterized by its unique combination of meat and vegetables, as well as its deep flavor profile from various spices and seasonings. This hearty goulash is often prepared outdoors over an open fire or in a large cauldron. Traditionally made with beef, Bograch can also include other meats such as pork or lamb. The main ingredients in Bograch include meat, onions, tomatoes, various types of paprika (sweet and hot), potatoes, and other seasonal vegetables. Ketchup or tomato paste is also an important component, adding richness to the dish. When cooking Bograch, large clay pots or cast-iron cauldrons are traditionally used. The dish is cooked slowly, allowing all the flavors to meld together beautifully. Once Bograch is ready, it is served hot, often garnished with fresh herbs.

Here's a step-by-step recipe for making classic Ukrainian borscht:

  • 500g beef, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2-3 bell peppers (paprika) or 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sweet paprika
  • 1 teaspoon hot paprika (adjust to taste)
  • 2-3 zucchinis, sliced into rings
  • 1-2 tomatoes, diced
  • 2-3 tablespoons ketchup
  • 1-2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped

Prepare the ingredients:

  • Cut the beef into cubes.
  • Chop the onion, bell peppers, potatoes, and tomatoes.

Cook the base:

  • In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil and sauté the chopped onion until golden brown.
  • Add the beef cubes and sauté until browned.

Cook the vegetables:

  • Add the diced potatoes, zucchinis, and tomatoes.
  • Pour in enough water to cover the vegetables.
  • Stir in the ketchup and mix thoroughly.
  • Simmer on medium heat for about 30-40 minutes or until the potatoes are tender.

Serve:

  • Remove the bay leaves.
  • Garnish with fresh chopped parsley.
  • Serve Bograch hot.

Enjoy your meal!
