Borscht is a traditional Ukrainian soup that is also widely popular in other Eastern and Central European countries such as Poland, Belarus, and others. It is a dish with a rich history and various preparation variations. The main ingredients in borscht typically include red or white cabbage, beets, potatoes, carrots, onions, tomato paste, and meat (usually beef or pork). The distinctive flavor of borscht comes from beets, which give the soup its characteristic red color. Borscht can be either "green" (without tomato paste) or "red" (with tomato paste). There are also many regional variations of borscht, which may include different ingredients or have their own unique preparation methods. To prepare borscht, the ingredients are usually chopped and boiled in broth. Adding salt, pepper, and other spices enhances the flavor of the dish. Sour cream and fresh herbs are often served with borscht. This dish has deep roots in the culture and traditions of Ukrainians and other communities. Borscht symbolizes hospitality and is often present on festive tables.

Here's a step-by-step recipe for making classic Ukrainian borscht:

  • 1 large beet
  • 1 large beet
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 onion
  • 1/2 cabbage (red or white)
  • 2-3 tomatoes or 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 200g meat (beef or pork), as preferred
  • 2-3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 4-5 black peppercorns
  • 2-3 tablespoons vinegar or lemon juice (optional)

Prepare the ingredients:

  • Clean and chop the meat into small pieces.
  • Peel and chop the vegetables: beet, potato, carrot, onion, and cabbage.
  • Peel the tomatoes (if using) and chop them into cubes.

Prepare the broth:

  • In a large pot, heat the oil and sauté the meat until golden brown.
  • Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  • Add the chopped carrot and tomatoes (or tomato paste) and sauté for 5-7 minutes.
  • Add the chopped potato and cabbage.
  • Pour in enough water to cover the ingredients. Add bay leaves and peppercorns.
  • Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer, covered, for approximately 30-40 minutes or until the potatoes are tender.

Prepare the vegetables for borscht:

  • In a separate pan, heat oil and sauté the chopped beet until tender. Add vinegar or lemon juice to preserve the beet's color.
  • Add the sautéed beet to the broth and cook for an additional 10-15 minutes

Seasoning and serving:

  • Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  • Remove the bay leaves and serve the borscht hot, garnished with fresh herbs and a dollop of sour cream.

Enjoy your meal!
